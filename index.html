MindBridge

About Features Support Open Chat

Youth-focused mental health support

Early support through a safe, empathetic AI chat experience.

MindBridge is a web-based mental health support system designed for young people. It offers a calm space to talk, identifies possible signs of distress, and signposts users to trusted organisations such as the NHS, Samaritans, and YoungMinds.

What this system does
  • Supports open conversation in a non-judgemental format
  • Assesses language for low, medium, or high concern
  • Provides supportive responses and trusted referrals
Important

This system is for early support only. It is not a crisis service, therapist, or diagnosis tool.

Project purpose

Why this matters

Many young people experience anxiety, depression, stress, isolation, or suicidal thoughts, but delay seeking help because of stigma, long waits, or uncertainty about where to go. This project explores how AI can support earlier help-seeking in a more accessible format.

Project boundaries

What this system does not do

  • Does not provide a medical diagnosis
  • Does not replace trained professionals
  • Does not offer emergency intervention
  • Does not store long-term personal histories in this demo version

Core features

Include:

AI conversation engine

Combines a guided safety prompt with risk analysis to produce calm, relevant, supportive responses.

Risk-aware support

Detects concerning phrases and changes the response pathway for higher-risk conversations.

Trusted referral links

Surface real organisations and helplines when a user needs further support beyond the chatbot.

Professional presentation

Responsive layout, cleaner branding and accessibility improvements.
